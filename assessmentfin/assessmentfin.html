This entire semester has been one of the most transformative experiences I have had as a design student. Coming into this class, I looked at interfaces and interactions in a surface-level way, but the frameworks we studied completely changed how I see design. Learning about Norman's principles of affordance and feedback, Nielsen's heuristics for usability, and Shneiderman's Golden Rules gave me a real vocabulary and a structured way of thinking that I know I will carry into every project going forward. These are not just theories I read about. They are tools I now genuinely use to make better design decisions.

I am also deeply grateful to Professor Oakley for pushing us to think analytically and not just aesthetically. The way this course was structured taught me to question every decision I make as a designer and to always put the user first. That shift in perspective has been incredibly valuable.

And to my classmates: Thank you. You were kind, encouraging, and genuinely helpful throughout every critique and work session. Getting feedback from people who were going through the same process made everything feel less intimidating and a lot more fun. I am really glad I got to learn alongside all of you.
