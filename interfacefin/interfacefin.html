Final Flow Reflection

The sketchbook started as a folder of files. Seventeen links with no context, no hierarchy, and nothing that told you what any of it was before you clicked. The audit made that impossible to keep ignoring, so this prototype became the attempt to fix that. Not visually. Structurally.

I designed mobile-first because that is how most people are going to look at this. On their phone, probably quickly. Designing for desktop first and shrinking it down does not actually solve anything. Starting small forced me to decide what actually needed to be there and what did not.

The three things I feel most resolved about are the groupings, the navigation, and the breadcrumbs. Splitting the work into Sketches, Projects, and Reflections means the shape of the semester is visible before you click anything. The breadcrumb means you always know where you are. The previous and next links mean no page is a dead end anymore.

Norman would say the interface is finally communicating something intentional rather than just existing. Nielsen would say the recognition problem is addressed because there are descriptors under every entry now. You do not have to remember what Number 4 was. Shneiderman would point to the homepage and say the memory load is finally reduced.

What still feels unresolved is efficiency. The structure works but getting to the actual content still takes longer than it should. That gap between landing on the page and reaching the thing you came for is something I want to close. It is the part I am not done thinking about.
