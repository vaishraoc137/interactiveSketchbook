Micro-Project Ideas

Uneven Blooms

Behavior: Not all happiness grows at the same rate, even with the same nurturing environment.

Interaction Rule: Two flowers receive the same “sunlight” input when clicked. One flower blooms steadily with each interaction, while the other only blooms intermittently, sometimes stalling or growing slower despite identical care. Over time, the fullness of the blooms are uneven.

Input: Clicks represent nurturing attention.

What changes over time: Petal count, flower size, and bloom fullness.

Where does friction appear: One flower responds irregularly or slowly despite identical care.

Intended Feeling: Joy (and longing?).

Energy Streams

Behavior: Some efforts feel effortless while others drain energy, even in the same environment.

Interaction Rule: Two leaves absorb sunlight when a key is pressed. One leaf retains energy steadily and accumulates glow, while the other slowly leaks energy whenever the key is released, requiring repeated effort to maintain brightness. Over time, one leaf thrives while the other struggles to keep up.

Input: Holding or pressing a key.

What changes over time: Leaf brightness, energy accumulation, or glow intensity.

Where does friction appear: Continuous energy leakage affects only one leaf.

Intended Feeling: Exhaustion + persistence.

Fleeting Nectar

Behavior: Rewards appear unevenly for different bees, creating anxiety.

Interaction Rule: Two bees try to collect nectar from flowers that bloom repeatedly. One bee always finds nectar when it arrives, while the other can only access nectar randomly. Over time, the first bee accumulates more nectar, while the second misses many chances due to timing constraints.

Input: Mouse movement or clicks.

What changes over time: Nectar collected by each bee.

Where does friction appear: Nectar availability is uneven, creating missed opportunities for one bee.

Intended Feeling: Anxiety.
