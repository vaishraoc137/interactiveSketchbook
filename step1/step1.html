Micro-Project Progress Report #2

1. Current Build

The system begins with two small brown seeds planted in the ground. When the user clicks, both seeds grow upward into flowers. One flower grows at a steady, fixed rate, while the other grows unpredictably using random values. The main variable controlling the system is height. As the height increases, visible state change occurs as stems extend and pink blossoms appear once they reach maximum growth.

2. Where Is the Pressure?

Pressure in the system is accumulative. Each click adds growth, pushing both flowers closer to their maximum height. The tension comes from the difference in pacing. The steady flower progresses predictably, while the random flower may surge ahead or lag behind. The contrast creates a visual imbalance that builds over time.

3. One Structural Addition This Week.

This week, I added a new visual element to represent growth stages. As the flowers grow, they now change color from brown to green for the stem and pink for the blossoms. This addition provides clearer feedback on the growth process and enhances the visual appeal of the system.
